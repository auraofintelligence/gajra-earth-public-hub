Actions

Make the first step obvious.

People should not need to understand the whole civilisation-scale architecture to help. They should be able to choose one honest action and leave a useful trace.

Action ladder

From ten minutes to a local circle.

Each rung is small enough to start, but structured enough to feed the larger public memory.

10 minutes

Write one definition

Answer: what does joyful responsible abundance look like where you live?

1 hour

Record one C-Hour

Do one useful public-good task and write down what happened, who benefits and what proof can be shared.

1 day

Host a values table

Invite a few people to define shared values, practical needs and one action the group can attempt.

1 month

Form a local circle

Repeat the table, publish notes, check boundaries and connect with another place or project.

Roles

Many ways to contribute.

GAJRA Earth should feel open without pretending everyone has the same role, risk level or authority.

Contributor

Shares a definition, action card, public note, hour, artwork, story, local need or useful link.

Host

Runs a small table, gathering, workshop, festival corner or online circle with clear consent.

Steward

Checks claims, boundaries, safety notes, aftercare, cultural protocols and public wording.

Researcher

Turns long reports into readable public summaries with source trails and caveats.

Builder

Improves pages, forms, ledgers, event templates, data exports and accessibility.

Sponsor

Funds practical public goods, but does not buy control of the commons memory.

Simple rules

Useful, calm and honest.

The early movement grows by lowering the threshold for good action while raising the standard for truth.

No false authority

Do not claim official partnership, government support, clinical approval or token value unless it exists.

No extraction

Keep local benefit, cultural respect, consent and aftercare ahead of attention harvesting.

No fog

Every public action should say what happened, what changed and what still needs work.
